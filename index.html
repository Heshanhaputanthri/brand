GDP per Capita

Which countries have the strongest Economies?


Gross domestic product (GDP) is the total monetary or market value of all the finished goods and services produced within a country over a certain time period. It is an indicator of the economies size and performance. GDP per Capita is the GDP per indivisual within the country. It is a useful metric to make cross-country comparisons of average living standards.

GDP per Capita strongly correlates with a number of metrics and indicaotrs which measure the general living standards of an economy such as life expectancy, happiness and freedom to maake life choices.

Are countries with higher GDP per Capita Happier?

There is a strong correlation between GDP per Capita, happiness as well as freedom to make life choices.

Countries with higher GDP per Capita tend to be countries with lower rates of unemployment which also plays a role in the metrics explored in the graphs.

This is mainly due to the increased disposable income as a result of increased per capita income.



Which continents have the strongest Economies?

Higher levels of GDP per Capita are associated with higher life expectancies. European countries tend to have high GDP per Capita due to the abundance of Tourism, Human Capitan and Technology.

African countries tend to deal with military dictatorships, corruption, civil unrest, underdevelopment and deep poverty which impacts the continents slow economic growth

Countries in the Asian continent tend to perform better than African countries on avergae due to the absense of deep poverty however do not perform as well as European countries due to the lack of Tourism and cutting edge Technology
