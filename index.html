GDP per Capita

Which countries perform the best?


Gross domestic product (GDP) is the total monetary or market value of all the finished goods and services produced within a country over a certain time period. It is important to measure GDP as it is an indicator of the economies size and performance. GDP per Capita is the GDP divided by the population of the country. It is a useful unit to make cross-country comparisons of average living standards.

GDP per Capita strongly correlates with a number of metrics and indicaotrs which measure the general living standards of an economy. Lets explore these metrics through some visualisations.

Are countries with Higher GDP per Capita Happier?

It is seen that there is a strong correlation between GDP per Capita and Happiness as well as life freedom. Countries with higher GDP per Capita tend to be countries with lower rates of unemployment which also plays a role in the metrics explored in the graphs.

This is mainly due to the increased disposable income as a result of increased per capita income.

Countries in the Asian continent tend to perform better than African countries on avergae due to the absense of deep poverty however do not perform as well as European countries due to the lack of Tourism and cutting edge Technology
